Savor the Flavors of Vigan

Vigan City is not only famous for its historical and cultural heritage but also for its unique and delicious local cuisine. The city's food is a reflection of its rich culinary traditions and heritage. Here are some of the must-try dishes and food items in Vigan City:

Vigan Longganisa:
Vigan is renowned for its distinct type of longganisa, which is a Filipino sausage. Vigan longganisa is characterized by its garlicky flavor and slightly sweet taste. It's often served with rice and eggs and is a staple for breakfast in the region.

Vigan Empanada:
Vigan empanadas are deep-fried pastries filled with a mixture of ground pork, vegetables, and sometimes green papaya. They are known for their bright orange color, which is achieved by using annatto seeds in the dough. A dipping sauce made from vinegar, garlic, and onions complements the empanadas perfectly.

Sinanglao:
Sinanglao is a traditional Ilocano soup made with beef or carabao meat, bile, and a variety of spices. It has a rich and savory flavor and is often enjoyed with a side of fish sauce and calamansi (a type of citrus).

Pakbet:
Pakbet, short for "Pinakbet," is a popular Ilocano vegetable dish made with a mix of locally grown vegetables such as bitter melon, okra, eggplant, and string beans, cooked with shrimp paste (bagoong) for a unique umami flavor.

Bagnet:
Bagnet is a crispy deep-fried pork belly dish. It's similar to lechon kawali but typically fried to a much crispier consistency. The crunchy skin and tender meat make it a favorite among locals and tourists alike.

Poqui-Poqui:
Poqui-poqui is a dish made from roasted and mashed eggplants cooked with tomatoes, onions, and beaten eggs. It's a vegetarian dish with a smoky and flavorful taste.

Bibingka:
Bibingka is a traditional Filipino rice cake that's often served in Vigan. It's made from rice flour, coconut milk, and sometimes cheese, and it's typically cooked in a clay pot or banana leaves over hot coals.

Miki:
Vigan miki is a type of noodle soup with a savory broth. It typically includes egg noodles, vegetables, and a flavorful broth. It's a comforting dish enjoyed by many in Vigan.

Tinubong:
Tinubong is a sticky rice cake made from glutinous rice, coconut milk, and sugar, often cooked inside bamboo tubes. It has a unique presentation and a sweet, coconut flavor.

When visiting Vigan, make sure to explore the local food markets, eateries, and restaurants to savor the unique and delicious flavors of the city. Vigan's cuisine is not only a treat for your taste buds but also a window into the rich culinary heritage of the Philippines.
